Teaching

Courses and student supervision at the University of Amsterdam.

Courses

Trustworthy AI for Business and Society

Teaching assistant · Led by Ilker Birbil · University of Amsterdam

This course covers fairness, accountability, transparency, and explainability in AI systems, with a focus on practical applications in business and societal contexts. My role went beyond standard TA work: I was responsible for designing the exam and resit, delivering tutorials, and supporting students throughout the course.

Interpretability and Explainability in AI

Teaching assistant · Led by Willem Zuidema · University of Amsterdam

This course studies techniques to ensure interpretability of modern AI methods and to generate explanations for classifications, decisions, and predictions. Topics range from post hoc interpretation techniques such as saliency maps, SHAP, integrated gradients, and attention-based methods, to approaches that build interpretability in by design, including neurosymbolic models and symbolic regression. I served as teaching assistant and supervised student group projects, which concluded in a poster session.

Applied Forecasting in Complex Systems

Teaching assistant · Led by Erman Acar · University of Amsterdam

This course covers time series analysis and forecasting, teaching students when to apply which method, which model is appropriate in which situation, and how the different approaches connect within the broader framework of data science and analytics. I served as student assistant and later took on supervision of student projects.

Information and Data Management

Teaching assistant · Led by Chintan Amrit · University of Amsterdam

This course provides a deeper understanding of key information technologies and data science concepts, with a focus on their application in business contexts spanning marketing, logistics, strategy, and entrepreneurship. Tutorials covered the basics of programming with Python and data science. I served as teaching assistant.
